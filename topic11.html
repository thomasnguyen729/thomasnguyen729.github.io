Topic 11 Review

Describe how vaccines work. [6]

  • inject weakened pathogen
  • macrophages engulf pathogens + presents antigen on its MHC to helper T-cell
  • T-cells release cytokines that activate B-cells to produce memory cells
  • On 2nd exposure, memory cells differentiate into plasma cells that make antibodies

Outline how monoclonal antibodies are produced. [3]

  • antibodies generated in lab
  • pathogens injected into animal which makes B-cells
  • specific B-cells are cultured with tumor cells to form hybrid cells that make antibodies which are harvested

State the function of tendons and ligaments. [2]

  • Tendons connect muscle to bone
  • Ligaments connect bone to bone

Outline the process of muscle contraction. [3]

  • Nerve impulses trigger the sarcoplasmic reticulum to release calcium ions which bind to troponin which move the tropomyosin out of the way so myosin heads can bind to actin fibers to create cross bridges. ATP binds to myosin which causes it to slide actin toward center of sarcomere.

Describe ultrafiltration. [2]

  • The afferent arterioles create pressure that pushes fluid through fenestrated capillaries into the Bowman's capsule. (Glomerus = arteries)

Describe selective reabsorption. [2]

  • The proximal convoluted tubule has many microvilli that increase absorption surface area to reabsirb glucose + amino acids

Describe osmoregulation. [2]

  • The descending loops of Henle reabsorbs water by osmosis and the ascending loops reabsorb salts trough active transport (ATP) or facilitated diffusion (membrane protein)

Outline spermatogenesis. [4]

  • Germline epithelium divide by mitosis to make spermatogonia
  • Spermatogonia divide by meiosis to make spermatids (relatively equal size)
  • Spermatids differentiate into functional spermatazoa which are released into the lumen of the tubule
  • Developing spermatozoa are nourished by Sertoli cells

Outline oogenesis. [4]

  • Oogonia undergo cell growth until large enough to undergo meiosis to become primary oocytes but are arrested in prophase I when granulosa cells surround them to form follicles.
  • FSH triggers division (puberty) of two cells of unequal size. Polar body and secondary oocyte which retains all cytoplasm.
  • Secondary oocyte is released from the ovary (ovulation) and enters the oviduct (fallopian tube)
  • Follicular cells surrounding the oocyte form a coronoa radiata which provides nourishment
